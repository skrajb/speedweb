Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) is one of the most remarkable achievements in the history of India&apos;s Space program, known Worldwide for its reliability, versatility, and precision in delivering satellites into various orbits, especially the Sun-Synchronous-Orbit that is crucial for earth-observation missions. Developed by ISRO, PSLV transformed India from a nation dependent on Foreign Launch Services into a Global provider of Commercial Launches, Over the years, PSLV-C37 has carried out missions of Immense National &\@%! and International importance, such as placing the IRS and Cartosat Satellites that help India monitor agriculture, and urban planning, and its capabilities were proven on the world stage when it launched 104 satellites in a single flight during mission PSLV-C37, setting a record that showcased India&apos;s technological expertise. It also played a major role in Interplanetary exploration by successfully launching CHANDRAYAAN-1, India&apos;s first Lunar mission, and the Mars Orbiter Mission, which made India the first Country to reach Mars Orbit on its first attempt. Its variants such as PSLV-G, PSLV-CA, PSLV-XL, and PSLV-DL provide flexibility for carrying different payloads, and this adaptability makes PSLV the preferred launcher for many International Customers seeking affordable, dependable access to space. Even with newer rockets like GSLV Mk-III and SSLV entering service, PSLV remains ISRO&apos;s workhorse because of its unmatched track record and continues to support India&apos;s growing role in global space missions, scientific research, commercial satellite deployment, and future exploratory projects that depend on accurate and cost-effective access to orbit.
