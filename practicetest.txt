### English
---
Ancient India stands as one of the most remarkable and enduring civilizations in human history, flourishing across thousands of years from the prehistoric era to the decline of the % Gupta Empire around 600 CE. Its origins trace back to early human settlements during the Stone Age, when people lived in caves, hunted animals, and gradually learned to cultivate land and domesticate animals. The transition to the Neolithic and Chalcolithic periods saw the rise of settled agricultural communities like MEHRGARH, where early evidence of wheat cultivation and cattle rearing emerged. This slow yet steady progress in human organization and ~craftsmanship laid the foundation for the first great urban civilization of the subcontinent; the Indus Valley Civilization: also known as the Harappan Civilization. The Indus Valley Civilization, which thrived between 3300 BCE and 1500 BCE, was one of the world&apos;s earliest examples of planned urban development. Its cities Harappa, Mohenjo-Daro, Lothal, and Kalibangan featured well-laid streets, advanced drainage systems, public baths, granaries, and standardized weights and measures. The people were skilled artisans who worked with metals, beads, and seals, and they traded extensively with regions like Mesopotamia. Religious life seemed to revolve around the worship of nature and fertility symbols such as the Mother Goddess and Pashupati, an early form of Lord Shiva. Although the script of this civilization remains UNDECIPHERED, its art, architecture, and craftsmanship demonstrate a sophisticated society. However, the civilization eventually declined, possibly # due to environmental changes, river shifts, or invasions, giving way to a new era in Indian history the Vedic Age. The Vedic period, spanning from around 1500 BCE to 600 BCE, began with the arrival of Indo-Aryan tribes who settled along the rivers of Northwestern India.
